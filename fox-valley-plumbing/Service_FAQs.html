What is backflow testing and why do I need it?
Backflow testing ensures your backflow prevention device is working properly to prevent contaminated water from flowing back into the clean water supply. Illinois requires annual testing for all commercial properties and residential irrigation systems to protect public health.
How often do I need backflow testing?
Backflow preventers must be tested annually by a certified tester. You'll receive a notice from your municipality when testing is due. We handle the testing and submit all required paperwork directly to your city.
What types of backflow devices do you service?
We test, repair, and install all types including RPZ (Reduced Pressure Zone), PVB (Pressure Vacuum Breaker), Double Check Valves, and Atmospheric Vacuum Breakers. Our technicians are Illinois-certified backflow testers.

What's the difference between drain cleaning and hydro jetting?
Drain cleaning uses mechanical cables (snakes) to break through clogs. Hydro jetting uses high-pressure water (3,000-4,000 PSI) to completely clean pipe walls, removing grease, scale, and debris. Hydro jetting provides a more thorough, longer-lasting clean.
What are signs I need drain cleaning?
Warning signs include: slow drains, gurgling sounds, bad odors, water backing up, multiple clogged drains, or fruit flies near drains. Don't wait until you have a complete blockage!
How often should I have my drains professionally cleaned?
For preventive maintenance, we recommend professional cleaning every 1-2 years. Restaurants and commercial kitchens may need more frequent service due to grease buildup.
üí° Pro Tip: Avoid chemical drain cleaners! They can damage pipes and often don't solve the underlying problem. Professional cleaning is safer and more effective.

How long does a water heater last?
Traditional tank water heaters typically last 8-12 years. Tankless water heaters can last 15-20 years with proper maintenance. Age, water quality, and maintenance all affect lifespan.
When should I repair vs. replace my water heater?
Consider replacement if: the unit is over 10 years old, you're seeing rust-colored water, there's water pooling around the base, or repair costs exceed 50% of replacement. We'll give you honest recommendations.
What's the difference between tank and tankless water heaters?
Tank heaters store 40-80 gallons of hot water, cost less upfront, but use more energy. Tankless heaters heat water on demand, are more energy-efficient (save 20-30%), but cost more initially. We can help you choose the right option.
How long does water heater installation take?
Standard tank water heater replacement takes 2-4 hours. Tankless installations may take 4-8 hours depending on whether gas line or electrical modifications are needed.

How do I know if my sump pump is working?
Test it by pouring a bucket of water into the pit. The pump should turn on, remove the water, then shut off. You should also hear it run during heavy rains. Test quarterly to ensure it's ready when you need it.
Do I need a battery backup sump pump?
Yes, we highly recommend it! Power outages often happen during severe storms‚Äîexactly when you need your sump pump most. A battery backup provides peace of mind and can prevent thousands in water damage.
How long do sump pumps last?
Average lifespan is 7-10 years, but this varies based on usage frequency and maintenance. If your pump runs frequently or is over 7 years old, consider replacement before it fails.

What are signs of a gas leak?
Warning signs include: rotten egg smell, hissing sounds near gas lines, dead plants near gas lines, higher gas bills, or physical symptoms (headaches, dizziness). If you suspect a leak, leave immediately and call us or 911.
Do you need a permit for gas line work?
Yes. Gas line installation and major repairs require permits and inspections in Illinois. As a licensed contractor, we handle all permit requirements and ensure work meets code.
Can you install gas lines for new appliances?
Absolutely! We install gas lines for stoves/ranges, dryers, outdoor grills, fire pits, pool heaters, and generators. We'll ensure proper sizing and safe installation.
üîç Leak Detection ‚ñº
How do you find hidden leaks?
We use electronic leak detection equipment that can locate leaks behind walls, under floors, and underground without destructive digging. This technology pinpoints the exact location, minimizing repair costs.
What are signs of a hidden water leak?
Watch for: unexplained high water bills, water meter running when nothing's on, wet spots on floors/walls, mold or mildew smell, foundation cracks, or sound of running water when fixtures are off.
How much water can a small leak waste?
A dripping faucet can waste 3,000+ gallons per year. A running toilet can waste 200 gallons per day! Hidden slab leaks can waste even more while causing structural damage. Early detection saves money.
